CLAIM REJECTIONS — 35 U.S.C. §112

Claims 1-8 are rejected under 35 U.S.C. §112(b) as indefinite.

Claim 1 recites "a cache management module configured to evict entries based on
predicted access recency." The claim recites a purely functional limitation — a
"module configured to" perform a stated result — without reciting the structure,
material, or acts that perform that function. Under §112(f) this limitation is
construed to cover the corresponding structure described in the specification and
its equivalents. The specification describes the desired outcome (entries with the
furthest predicted next access are evicted) but discloses no algorithm by which
the prediction is computed.

Where a claimed function is performed by a general-purpose computer, the
specification must disclose the algorithm that transforms it into a special
purpose computer. Absent such disclosure the claim is indefinite.

RESPONSE OF RECORD (entered 2019-03-04)

Applicant amended claim 1 to recite the specific steps by which the prediction is
computed — maintaining a recency histogram per cache line, applying a decay factor
at a fixed interval, and selecting the line whose decayed score is lowest — and
added corresponding support at spec paragraphs [0044]-[0051]. The §112(b)
rejection was withdrawn.
